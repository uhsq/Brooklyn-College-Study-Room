About the Library

Librarian
Mary Mallery

Welcome to the Brooklyn College Library! The Brooklyn College Library is one of the largest academic libraries in New York State and serves over 17,000 students, faculty, and staff. As part of the City University of New York (CUNY) system, the library collaborates with other CUNY libraries to support over 550,000 students across the nationâ€™s largest urban public university.

The library offers a mix of tradition and modernity. Housed in a beautiful Georgian-style building, the structure combines elements from its original 1939 design, a 1959 addition, and a major renovation in 2002 that expanded its size by 72,000 square feet. Inside, the LaGuardia Reading Room features historic murals from the Works Progress Administration (WPA).

A special feature is the Archives and Special Collections Division, which includes rare collections like the Robert L. Hess Collection on the Horn of Africa and the Kaplan Boxing Archive, valued at $3 million. New additions include papers from renowned Brooklyn College alumnus Alan Dershowitz.

The library staff is committed to providing excellent resources and services to support learning and research. From online literacy tutorials to continuous improvements, the Brooklyn College Library stays at the forefront of academic innovation.
